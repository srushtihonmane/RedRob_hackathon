- `skill_assessment_scores`: 24.2% coverage; CV/speech assessments common (~1.1k each), IR/PEFT/vector rare (~340) = high-signal. Corroboration gold + cv_speech negative evidence.

**D2 ‚Äî Dense-embedding text composition (LOCKED): two vectors per candidate.**
- **Identity embedding** = `current_title` + `headline` + `summary` (what the candidate presents as; captures title signal, current positioning, pivots, stuffer behavior). One vector, passage prefix, L2-norm.
- **Evidence embedding** = what they have actually done. Build a per-role doc (title+description), embed each role offline, compute `role_relevance = cosine(role_emb, jd_ideal)` (Goal 2 frozen ideal). **Always keep all role titles** (high-signal, low-token); spend the remaining ~480-token budget on the **highest-relevance** role descriptions (query-aware, **not** chrono). Assemble one evidence doc (all titles + top-relevance descriptions) ‚Üí one final evidence embedding.
- *Rejected:* chronological truncation; pure per-role embeddings. *Free byproducts ‚Üí features:* role-relevance summaries (G9), `identity_evidence_divergence` (G10) as an orthogonal stuffer/pivot signal. Query-aware selection is legal (singular fixed offline JD) and makes the evidence cosine meaningful **comparatively** ‚Äî Goal 4 owns absolute scale/calibration. Deterministic assembly order: titles chronological, then descriptions relevance-descending with candidate_id/role-index tie-break.

**D3 ‚Äî BM25/lexical doc (LOCKED): narrative-only corpus + prebuilt index.**

- Tokenization: lowercase alphanumeric unigrams + **controlled phrase-joining** from Goal 2's lexical concept vocabulary (preserves "information retrieval", "vector database", "learning to rank", "sentence transformers" as single tokens, both corpus & query side).
- Ship prebuilt offline: `bm25_index/` = CSR doc-term `.npz` + `idf.npy` + `doclen.npy` + `vocab.json`; runtime scores fixed JD query terms via one sparse mat-vec. Corpus = full 100k. Additive-positive (absence never penalizes) enforced at Goal 4 scoring, not in the artifact.
